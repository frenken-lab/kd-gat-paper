---
title: "Adaptive Fusion of Graph-Based Ensembles for Automotive Intrusion Detection"
subtitle: "PhD Candidacy Report"
abstract: |
  Controller Area Network (CAN) intrusion detection in modern vehicles must operate under diverse attack types, severe class imbalance, and strict hardware constraints. We present a multi-stage, multi-expert ensemble graph framework that models CAN traffic using structural, temporal, and distributional cues. A Variational Graph Autoencoder (VGAE) learns normal graph structure and guides targeted training of a Graph Attention Network (GAT) classifier. A bandit-based policy adaptively fuses experts per sample. To enable deployment, a resource-aware knowledge-distillation (KD) pipeline compresses the ensemble into lightweight students, while curriculum imbalance training enhances rare-attack detection. Building on the current framework, we propose extensions including physics-informed anomaly validation (PINN), modular DQN ensemble scaling, intelligent knowledge distillation guided by hardware constraints and scaling laws, advanced explainable AI methods (LIME, SHAP, counterfactual analysis, TCAV, prototype-based learning), and cross-domain generalization to network IDS benchmarks beyond the automotive domain.
